Where We Are

The current product state.

Tether already behaves like a real guardrail tool for AI coding sessions. It is not formal verification, but it is a working workflow with real checks and real enforcement.

Live Now

Feature ledger and blast radius

Tether maps features to files, tracks dependencies, and runs `plan` before changes to estimate risk.

Live Now

Session drift and targeted checks

`watch` can monitor edits during a session and surface drift or targeted failures instead of waiting until the end.

Live Now

Confidence-gated verification

`verify` checks main tests and can block session completion when high-confidence must-handle edge cases are still failing.

Roadmap

What comes next.

These are the milestones that move Tether from a strong dogfooded tool into something we can talk about more boldly in public.

Next

Sharpen the generated edge cases

  • Reduce noisy or generic test suggestions
  • Make first-run bootstrap outputs cleaner for new repos
Next

  • Measure how often Tether catches regressions that would otherwise slip through

Stronger Claims

What we need before we can say more.

The right move is not to overclaim. The right move is to add the proof points that make stronger claims fair.

To claim better accuracy

  • Track prediction quality over time
  • Store false positive and false negative rates
  • Benchmark Tether on known regression scenarios

To claim stronger confidence

  • Replace heuristic confidence-only scoring with evidence from outcomes
  • Show that high-confidence cases fail more often when left unchecked
  • Publish examples where the gate caught real misses

To claim launch readiness at scale

  • Onboard outside users
  • Document successful runs on non-demo codebases
  • Tighten setup, docs, and first-run experience
